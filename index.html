Leading and Building a Culture of Financial Stability

Course Overwiew

In changing organization culture after pandemic, the ability to drive financial stability in public sector is more critical than ever. This lab-style leadership program gives you first-hand insight into how the organizational goals could be achieved and how you can foster the social and culture processes that enable and inspire organization-wide financial stability and agility.

Who Should Attend !

General managers who lead institution, business units, division, and other large-scale organizations; executives with cross-functional or change management responsibilities; and leaders of R&D, corporate strategy, financial, legal, human resources, marketing, manufacturing, customer services, and sales.

What You Will Learn !

. Considering creativity and innovation broadly as any new idea that can help your organization to improve financial stability

. Identifying the common practices in culture that can stifle the collaboration and innovation

. Understanding the leader’s role as social architect

. Becoming a change agent who can advocate for change at all levels

. Fulfilling three key leadership imperatives – managing yourself, your team, and your network – in ways that facilitate financial stability

. Growing contextual intelligence to compete and thrive in different cultural environments

. Developing an effective culture of new ideas for good governance and fair practices

. Institutionalizing experimentation, risk-taking and the tolerance for failure

. Designing spaces that support collaborative problem solving

. Evolving decision-making processes to align with the nature of innovation
